# Client Intake Guide

## Accepted Inputs

- website URL
- social profile URLs
- market scope hints (city, region, country, niche, product group)
- objective of the analysis or proposal
- competitor or reference brand URLs
- competitor or benchmark URLs
- notes about priority products or projects
- product/service details that should lead the cycle
- visual references or brand constraints
- the creative table for the client, if it already exists
- any existing proof, reviews, testimonials or cases
- PDF, DOCX, PPTX or TXT files
- catalogs, media kits, proposals and brochures
- product pages and service pages

## Recommended Intake Flow

1. Start with the website and 1-3 key social links.
2. Add product or service documents if available.
3. Include any notes about goals, audience or priority offers.
4. Let the system discover official profiles and produce the first social intelligence summary.
5. Let the system extract the first draft client record from the collected evidence.
6. Review the record and correct anything important.
7. Approve the final version before research and content planning continue.
8. If the cycle will include long-form content, carry that decision forward so the later blog, discovery and repurpose steps can be activated.
9. If the cycle may need a proposal, carry that decision forward so the proposal checkpoint can be asked at the right time.

## Extraction Goals

1. company identity
2. products and services
3. audience and market
4. market scope and competitor cues
5. business strategy and offer structure
6. tone of voice and positioning
7. proof points, cases and differentiators
8. visual identity cues and brand signals
9. market context and competitor cues
10. presence digital signals from site, blog and social channels

## Editing Rule

All extracted information must remain editable after the first pass.
When the user corrects something, the corrected version becomes the active client record for the squad.

## Source Priority

- Use the website for official positioning and offer structure.
- Use social profiles for tone, recurring themes and proof points.
- Use documents for product/service detail and commercial nuance.
- Use visual assets for palette, layout cues and motion style.
- Use the social intelligence summary for presence, frequency, gaps and confidence.
- Use the brand profile template for the generated fact/inference split.
- Use the creative table template to drive image, typography and layout decisions.
- Use user corrections to update the persisted database record; the database becomes the active source of truth for clients, products and services.
- After intake, the next research cycle must read the persisted client record before collecting new evidence.
- When the research changes the diagnosis, the strategy step must consult the persisted strategy library again.
- If the cycle is analytic only, do not move automatically into proposal building.

## Operational Rule

The intake should answer one question quickly:
"What does this client sell, to whom, in which market frame, and why should the audience care?"

# Brand Profile Template

## Purpose

Store the structured brand and offer intelligence that sits above social intelligence and feeds content, proposal and creative generation.

This layer may be partially inferred from the website and public web research, but it must always separate:
- confirmed facts
- inferred hypotheses
- missing confirmations
- assets still needed

## Confirmed Facts

- brand name
- website
- category
- product or service line
- offer priority
- primary CTA
- operating region
- price range, when public
- delivery model

## Audience Profile

- primary audience
- secondary audience
- buying context
- main pains
- desired outcomes
- objections
- awareness level
- language to use
- language to avoid

## Offer Profile

- main offer
- supporting offers
- product/service hierarchy
- unique value proposition
- differentiators
- promise
- proof points
- reasons to believe
- constraints or exclusions

## Visual and Brand Signals

- color palette
- typography cues
- visual style
- recurring motifs
- content format preference
- motion style preference

## Social Proof

- confirmed testimonials
- confirmed reviews
- public case studies
- before/after evidence
- process proof
- institutional proof
- creator or founder proof
- proof gaps

## Inferred Signals

- inferred audience
- inferred pains
- inferred desires
- inferred UVP
- inferred objections
- inferred proof themes
- inferred palette
- inferred tone of voice

## Confidence Tags

- confirmed
- inferred
- partial
- needs confirmation
- not available

## Usage Rule

The brand profile can be auto-generated from the initial research pass, but all social proof, claims and conversion promises must remain evidence-backed.
If the system cannot confirm a proof item, it must be marked as missing or inferred rather than invented.

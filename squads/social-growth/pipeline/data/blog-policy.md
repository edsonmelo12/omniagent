# Blog Policy

This policy applies to every client handled by the `social-growth` squad.
It defines the minimum editorial standard for long-form blog content regardless of niche, offer or audience.

## Policy Statement

- Every blog piece must defend a clear thesis.
- Every blog piece must choose an intentional structure family.
- Every blog piece must choose a family-specific section arc, not a default skeleton.
- Every blog piece must include proof, nuance and a visible answer path.
- Every blog piece must pass a proof freshness gate before drafting proceeds.
- Every blog piece must record a family ledger entry before final drafting.
- Every blog piece must declare a word-count target based on function, not on habit.
- Every blog piece must define a featured-image direction that reinforces the thesis.
- Every blog piece must define a featured-image selection rationale that justifies why the image won the criteria.
- Every published HTML must render the featured image successfully.
- Every published HTML must use final Portuguese orthography, with accents normalized.
- Every blog piece must have ≥30% of sentences containing a transition word (adição, contraste, causa, ordem, ênfase, exemplificação).
- Generic internal headings such as `Intro` and `Conclusion` must not appear in public HTML.
- Every blog piece must use schema only when the visible content supports it.
- Every blog piece must read like a finished editorial asset, not an expanded outline.

## Default Word Count Ranges

- 900 to 1,200 words for tactical or decision-oriented posts.
- 1,200 to 1,600 words for the default long-form standard.
- 1,700 to 2,200 words for pillar posts that require more proof or nuance.

These ranges are defaults, not absolute laws. If a topic needs a different range, the architecture must state why.

## Featured Image Rules

- The image must be thesis-led.
- The image must be specific to the article topic.
- The image must help explain the article in one glance.
- Generic stock imagery should be avoided unless the topic literally benefits from a literal scene.
- Conceptual, strategic or proof-led articles should prefer original or topic-specific visual treatments.
- Visual sourcing must be free or public by default; no paid stock unless explicitly approved.
- Selection must pass three checks: topic match, thesis match and one-glance clarity.
- The image should be rejected if it looks like a category illustration that could be reused across unrelated posts.

## Schema Rules

- Use `BlogPosting` only when the visible article is editorial content.
- Use `FAQPage` only when the FAQ appears visibly in the article.
- Use `Organization` and `WebSite` for entity pages, not generic blog posts.
- Never add schema to compensate for weak content.

## Rejection Conditions

Reject or rewrite the piece if any of the following are true:

- The thesis is vague or exchangeable.
- The structure family is generic and unintentional.
- The section arc is just the default blog mold in different clothing.
- The proof map is thin or purely decorative.
- The proof freshness gate is missing or the piece overclaims beyond available proof.
- The article feels like an outline inflated into prose.
- The word-count target is not aligned with the function of the piece.
- The featured image is generic or disconnected from the argument.
- The featured image fails to render in the final HTML.
- The public HTML exposes draft-style headings or unaccented final copy.
- The schema is decorative rather than supported by visible content.

## Acceptance Criteria

- The article proves a point, not only explains a topic.
- The article feels inevitable for the theme and not reusable as a generic market template.
- The image strengthens the editorial argument.
- The length matches the work the article has to do.
- The final reading experience is coherent, specific and publishable.
- The proof freshness gate is explicit and defensible.
- The family ledger entry is present and prevents nearby repetition.

# Daily Execution Checklist

## Before Starting

- Open the current client, product and strategy records in the database and confirm the current offer, audience and tone.
- Open the database-backed market research record only if you need a review copy.
- Open `output/strategy/content-plan.md` and confirm the week priority.
- Open the blog architecture, blog draft and discovery optimization outputs if the cycle includes long-form content, and confirm the word-count target, featured-image direction, featured-image selection criteria and source search note.
- Open `output/publishing/schedule-plan.md` and confirm what goes first.

## Content Production

- Confirm the platform and format for the day.
- Adapt the core idea to the native structure of that channel.
- If the asset comes from a blog, confirm the architecture, discovery optimization and repurpose outputs are approved, including the length target, thesis-led featured image, image selection rationale and source search note.
- Keep the hook specific and the CTA actionable.
- Avoid copying the same caption across all platforms.

## Publishing

- Confirm that `step-04b-pipeline-compliance-audit.md` has produced a `PASS` or `PASS_WITH_WARNINGS` report before asking for approval.
- Confirm that the compliance report includes `Integrity Checks` from `pipeline-integrity-gate.md` for any created, regenerated or corrected asset.
- Do not update final hub links, schedules, queues or publish manifests when the compliance report is `BLOCKED`, `INVALID` or missing.
- Do not call an asset `ready for publication` unless final export files exist, dimensions were validated, the hub/manifest points to the same canonical version and user approval is complete when scheduling/publishing is involved.
- Publish the highest-priority asset first.
- Review spelling, CTA and visual consistency before posting.
- Confirm that the post matches the approved tone.
- Save the final version used for publication.

## Monitoring

- Check saves, shares, comments and profile visits after publication.
- Record any strong feedback or recurring objections.
- Note whether the content generated replies, clicks or direct messages.
- Flag any post that clearly outperforms the rest.

## Client Update

- If the client changes an offer, update the client record first.
- If the client changes tone or positioning, update the client record first.
- If the client changes the blog angle, rerun blog architecture and discovery optimization before repurposing, and recheck the length, featured-image, image-selection and source-search constraints.
- If a post reveals a new topic of interest, record it for the next cycle.

## Weekly Wrap-Up

- Identify the best-performing theme of the week.
- Identify the weakest asset and why it underperformed.
- Decide whether the next cycle should repeat, adjust or retire the topic.

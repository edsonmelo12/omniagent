# Campaign Transition Workflow

## Purpose

Convert the previous campaign into the starting point for the next cycle, with no duplicate analysis and no lost learning.

## When to use

Use this workflow whenever a campaign closes, a weekly review ends, or the team needs to open a new cycle from existing evidence.

## Required Inputs

- Atos strategic report
- Action plan
- Trend opportunity radar
- Strategy feedback loop
- Campaign planning pack
- Campaign review pack
- Current client index or operational source of truth
- Current weekly theme or active editorial plan
- Client record and proof gaps
- Video intelligence brief and client adaptation layer when available

## Natural Steps

### 1. Read the previous cycle

- open the core strategy artifacts
- open the published or approved assets when needed
- group the output by channel and funnel stage
- if video intelligence exists, read the consolidated brief before extracting the next-cycle signal

### 2. Separate outcomes

- what worked
- what worked partially
- what failed because of execution
- what failed because of distribution
- what is still inconclusive

### 3. Extract the decision signal

- thesis
- mechanism
- proof
- objection
- CTA
- channel role
- confidence level

### 4. Update the memory layer

- move the confirmed signals into `scale`, `optimize`, `retest`, `pause`, or `archive`
- record false-negative suspects
- record what should not be repeated

### 5. Update the opportunity layer

- preserve strong angles
- keep under-tested angles visible
- remove weak angles from the next cycle unless there is a clear recovery path

### 6. Choose the next-cycle thesis

- one central thesis
- one primary objection
- one primary proof point
- one primary CTA

### 7. Map the role of each channel

- Blog: depth and decision
- Carousel: synthesis and criteria
- Reels: quick diagnosis and attention
- Stories: daily objection handling and intent capture
- Direct: assisted conversion

### 8. Build the new briefing

- what enters
- what exits
- what the cycle is trying to prove
- what each channel must do
- what metric confirms the thesis
- hand off into the mandatory campaign planning gate before strategy is locked

### 9. Check the guardrails

- no prohibited technical tutorial
- no generic copy without a clear criterion
- no CTA without funnel purpose
- no reuse of a weak angle without evidence

### 10. Close the handoff

- register the decision in the feedback loop
- refresh the operational index when needed
- hand the briefing to the next planning step
- if the cycle is ending, file the campaign review pack before the next planning gate starts
- preserve the adapted video intelligence chain in the handoff notes

## Decision Rules

- `scale` when the pattern is strong and repeatable.
- `optimize` when the direction is right but needs execution tuning.
- `retest` when the signal is promising but incomplete.
- `pause` when the cost is higher than the expected value.
- `archive` when the angle does not fit the client.

The transition workflow must always feed the campaign planning gate before the next strategy brief is finalized.

## Output

- consolidated read of the previous campaign
- updated decision portfolio
- next-cycle briefing
- confirmed starting point for the new campaign

## Efficiency Criterion

The workflow is efficient when the next campaign starts with a clear thesis, fewer weak repetitions, and less rework in briefing and production.

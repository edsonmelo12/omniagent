# Visual Direction

Client: active client
Cycle: current cycle
Primary channel: Instagram
Support channels: Facebook, Pinterest

## Visual System

### Mood
- editorial, practical and confident
- clean rather than decorative
- artisan-friendly without looking handmade in a casual way

### Design Rules
- 2 fonts max, but vary the treatment by role
- 4-color palette max
- strong contrast on every cover
- generous whitespace
- one dominant idea per slide or frame
- mobile-first readability
- proof must be visible, not implied
- avoid repeating the same typographic backbone across the cycle

### Recommended System
- Primary text color: near-black
- Background: warm off-white
- Accent 1: deep earthy red for hooks and alerts
- Accent 2: muted green for proof, support and routines

### Typography Hierarchy
- H1: large, compact, high-contrast hook
- H2: short support line or proof statement with a different rhythm from H1
- Body: short lines, no long paragraphs, use line breaks to create pace
- Proof: highlight the most credible phrase or number separately from the body
- CTA: visually isolated, compact and easy to spot
- For Amiclube educational image-led posts, use a working scale of 66px for the title, 40px for the complementary line, 37px for bullets, 40px for the CTA, and 35px for the footer line.
- Keep that scale as the default unless mobile readability or a deliberate hierarchy shift requires an exception.
- Secondary emphasis: use weight, spacing or case changes before adding another font.
- If a keyword must be emphasized inside the title, keep the title size stable and vary color or weight instead of shrinking the keyword.
- Emphasized keywords must stay inline with the phrase, not visually detached from adjacent words.
- If the highlighted word still collides with adjacent lines, move that keyword to its own continuation line inside the same title block rather than forcing it to share space with the previous line.
- When a client palette has a dark anchor, the title should use that palette color as the main text tone rather than a neutral black.
- Avoid white halos, white strokes, or bright drop shadows on body or title text; they read as rendering artifacts on light imagery.
- Any support line inside the artwork is part of the composition, not a caption; keep it at a readable body size and shorten the copy before shrinking the type. On mixed-tone backgrounds, prefer a darker image treatment plus light text, or move the whole text block onto a stable plate before shrinking the copy.
- Before selecting a source image, check niche fidelity explicitly: the image must match the client's exact craft or category, may use only adjacent signals that stay neutral, and must never read as a neighboring niche (for example, crochet must not look like knitting).
- These rules are client-agnostic; adapt palette, imagery, and copy length, but keep the hierarchy rules stable across posts.

### Contrast Rule
- Never place a light title directly on a light background without a local darkening layer.
- Prefer a gradient, translucent panel, or darker crop zone behind the title before changing the title size.
- If the image is bright, let the title sit on a controlled contrast field so readability stays stable on mobile.

### Visual Language
- use cards, steps, labels, highlights and small proof blocks
- prefer real product shots, process photos, catalog images and screenshots over abstract decorative art
- keep overlays minimal on reels and pins
- avoid generic corporate gradients and overly ornamental flourishes

### Image-Led Rule
- When the piece uses a support image, the image must occupy the full background canvas.
- Use a controlled scrim or gradient to protect legibility; do not replace the background with a solid side card or opaque panel.
- Keep the text block floating over the image with clear contrast and light typography.
- Only use a stable plate when the composition explicitly requires it; otherwise, the background must remain visible behind the text.
- If contrast is still weak, darken the image base first and then reapply a transparent scrim; do not let the overlay turn the composition into a black or opaque field.

## Instagram Feed

Feed is the anchor of the cycle. Every carousel should read like a small editorial story.

### Cover Pattern
- hook in 3 to 7 words
- high contrast
- one focal object or texture
- no more than 2 supporting words

### Slide Pattern
- Slide 1: promise or pain point
- Slides 2 to 4: explanation, example or proof
- Slide before CTA: practical takeaway
- Last slide: explicit CTA

### Feed Constraints
- one idea per slide
- avoid text blocks longer than 20 to 25 words
- use visual rhythm changes between proof, explanation and action
- never overload the cover with bullets

## Instagram Reels

Reels should feel direct, useful and fast.

### Cover Pattern
- 3 to 5 words max
- close crop, product or face
- one bright accent only
- strong readable type on small screens

### On-Screen Text
- keep overlays short
- use 1 idea per screen beat
- use captions to support, not replace, the video

### Visual Beats
- beat 1: hook
- beat 2: proof or product
- beat 3: explanation
- beat 4: practical close

### Reels Priority
- keep proof close to the hook
- avoid long intro shots
- do not bury the CTA at the end without a clear frame or spoken cue

## Instagram Stories

Stories should function as a lightweight support layer, not a separate campaign.

### Story Structure
- Frame 1: hook or question
- Frame 2: context or proof
- Frame 3: interaction or CTA

### Story Style
- minimal clutter
- large type
- one question or one proof element per frame
- use polls, questions or tap prompts when relevant

### Story Role in This Cycle
- reinforce the day’s hook
- show proof or process
- move people to reply, save or click through

## Facebook

Facebook is support distribution, not a separate creative system.

### Adaptation Rules
- shorten the caption
- reduce layout density
- keep the strongest educational frame or image
- favor clarity over decorative repetition

### Facebook Role
- redistribute the strongest message
- keep the page active
- support educational trust

## Pinterest

Pinterest should behave as evergreen discovery.

### Pin Style
- vertical composition
- title-led
- clean and save-worthy
- one strong takeaway per pin

### Pin Treatment
- strong headline at top
- supporting point in the middle
- small brand mark or subtle footer
- avoid tiny text and complex multi-column layouts

### Pinterest Role
- turn the most visual educational pieces into discovery assets
- preserve clarity over brand flourish

## Piece-by-Piece Direction

### 1. Pare de travar em receitas confusas
- Feed: editorial cover with high contrast and a hard hook.
- Visual focus: confusion vs clarity.
- Stories: question about the biggest selling struggle.

### 2. O que você recebe não é só uma receita
- Reels: product or process close-up.
- Visual focus: value beyond the surface.
- Support: use a proof detail or package shot.

### 3. Organização vende mais do que improviso
- Post: minimal checklist aesthetic.
- Visual focus: routine and structure.
- Facebook adaptation: shorter text with a clean list.

### 4. 3 dúvidas que travam sua venda
- Carousel: 3-card structure, one doubt per card.
- Visual focus: price, clarity, confidence.
- Stories: use one question sticker.

### 5. Bastidor que gera valor percebido
- Reel: hands, process, material and finishing.
- Visual focus: proof through making.
- Pinterest: crop the most visual process frame.

### 6. O erro que faz você vender menos
- Carousel: contrast or before/after format.
- Visual focus: weak presentation versus stronger presentation.
- Use a split layout and a single strong comparison.

### 7. O que aprendemos nesta semana
- Post: weekly recap card.
- Visual focus: summary and pattern recognition.
- Keep it simple and text-light.

### 8. Clareza comercial muda tudo
- Carousel: argument-driven layout.
- Visual focus: clarity, decision and friction reduction.
- Use a clean sequence with one claim per slide.

### 9. O valor real está no que evita
- Reel: fast cuts with a clear claim overlay.
- Visual focus: avoided pain and practical value.
- Do not make the cover abstract.

### 10. Como vender sem parecer confuso
- Post: bold typography and one key principle.
- Visual focus: simple selling language.
- Facebook should use the same visual but a shorter caption.

### 11. Suporte também é diferencial
- Carousel: proof, support and reassurance.
- Visual focus: trust through accompaniment.
- Use a supportive, calm visual rhythm.

### 12. Rotina simples para não travar
- Post: planner, checklist or flow structure.
- Visual focus: repeatable system.
- Pinterest version should feel like a practical saveable tip.

### 13. Prova que convence sem forçar
- Reel: proof-first framing.
- Visual focus: result or evidence close to the hook.
- Keep the cover minimal and specific.

### 14. A tese da campanha
- Carousel manifesto: strongest type hierarchy of the cycle.
- Visual focus: clear thesis statement.
- Final slide should close the cycle with the brand promise.

### Source Planning

For each piece, the visual brief should also state:

- source class
- whether an external source is needed
- the preferred source class when support imagery is required
- the source search note and rationale if a file is sourced externally

Use the source-decision matrix as the default planning tool.

### Review Checklist
- readable on a small phone screen
- hook visible in under 2 seconds
- no channel is using another channel’s layout unchanged
- proof element is visible when the post depends on credibility
- CTA is visually separated from the body

### Constraints
- avoid text-heavy covers
- avoid generic stock imagery
- avoid decorative elements that do not support the thesis
- keep every asset aligned with the same campaign system

### Execution Rule
- if a piece cannot be understood in one glance on mobile, simplify it before production.
- this brief is the input for the render step, not the final asset itself.

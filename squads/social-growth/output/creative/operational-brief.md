# Operational Brief

Client: active client
Cycle: current cycle
Primary channel: Instagram
Support channels: Facebook, Pinterest
Mechanism: template/render production, no AI image model for the first pass

## Objective

Build a repeatable organic cycle that positions the active client as a practical reference in its niche, with clear commercial language, visible proof and a simple routine.

## Strategic Frame

- Primary strategy: Autoridade para gerar prova
- Secondary strategy: Conversão para demonstrar valor
- Safety strategy: Plano B para reduzir risco

## Campaign Thesis

The client helps its audience with clarity, proof and routine.

## Channel Allocation

### Instagram
- Primary daily feed channel
- Main home of the campaign
- Carries the strongest hooks, proof and thesis slides

### Stories
- Daily support layer
- Reinforces hooks, proof and interaction
- Keeps the campaign warm between feed posts

### Facebook
- Redistribution channel
- Uses the strongest educational or proof frames
- Keeps the page active without creating a separate campaign

### Pinterest
- Evergreen discovery channel
- Uses the most save-worthy visuals
- Prioritizes clarity, title-led framing and vertical composition

### TikTok
- Inactive until live profile confirmation

## Visual Mechanism

- Use the approved visual direction as the base system.
- Keep the visual system editorial, practical and confident.
- Use the product when it increases proof, value or trust.
- Use text-first layouts when the piece depends on clarity or explanation.
- Use comparison or before/after when the piece depends on persuasion.

### Client Creative Direction Layer

For client batches, the visual direction must be filtered through the dedicated creative-director skill before it becomes production-ready.

- Load `skills/creative-director/SKILL.md`
- Apply `creative-rules.md` for brand behavior and quality thresholds
- Apply `art-directions.md` to pick a direction family per piece
- Apply `anti-repetition.md` to block repeated structures across adjacent items
- Use `test-prompts.md` when validating the batch direction before render
- Confirm the batch against [Creative Validation Checklist](validation-checklist.md) before render approval

## Production Mechanism

### What Creates the Pieces
- The `creator` writes the copy and platform variations.
- The `visual-director` turns the approved copy into production-ready visual briefs.
- The `creative-renderer` turns the brief into the final rendered asset.
- The final piece is produced through template/render workflow and recorded in a manifest.
- No AI image model is required for the first pass.

### What the Team Should Build
- Instagram master asset
- Render manifest for every post
- Facebook adaptation when needed
- Pinterest adaptation when the piece is save-worthy
- Stories support frames
- Reel cover and proof treatment

## Schedule Summary

The approved cycle runs from `2026-04-06` to `2026-04-19` with 14 daily feed pieces.

### Timing Pattern
- Monday to Sunday cadence
- 1 Instagram feed post per day
- Stories daily
- Facebook support on selected dates
- Pinterest support on selected dates
- Reels on the proof/value beats

### Support Distribution
- Facebook support dates: 1, 3, 4, 7, 10, 12
- Pinterest support dates: 2, 5, 8, 11, 14
- Reels positions: 2, 5, 9, 13

## Production Order

1. Build or approve the discovery-optimized blog when long-form content is in scope.
2. Derive the repurpose package from the blog when multichannel output is needed.
3. Build the Instagram master asset.
4. Derive the Facebook adaptation when needed.
5. Derive the Pinterest version when the piece is save-worthy.
6. Run a mobile readability review.
7. Approve for scheduling only after the visual check passes.

## Final Guardrails

- Do not use AI image generation on the first pass.
- Do not create a separate creative system per channel.
- Do not use product images where they do not add proof.
- Do not ship a layout that is not readable on mobile.
- Do not separate copy, visual direction, render and execution into disconnected artifacts.
- For any external image, record the source class, source search note and rationale before render.

# Creative DNA - Amiclube

## Scope

Client: Amiclube
Use this DNA as the default creative frame for the brand and its organic batches.

## Brand Reading

- Segment: amigurumi, artesanato and education for artisans
- Tone: close, practical, instructive and encouraging
- Core promise: help artisans sell more with content, clarity and routine
- Brand behavior: warm, helpful, tactile, real-world, non-corporate

## Creative DNA

- Brand feel: warm, expressive and supportive
- Mood: artesanal, colorful and human
- Energy level: medium-high
- Creative role: teaching plus confidence building
- Visual identity: decorative but controlled

## Palette Anchors

- Base: warm neutrals, cream and soft dark contrast
- Accents: gold, amber, coral, pink and teal as campaign accents
- Rule: keep the palette lively, but never noisy

## Image Strategy

- Preferred image story: hands, materials, routines, products, workspace mood and close human context
- Preferred image types: support photo, craft process, product close-up, decorative environment, lifestyle detail
- Best use of background image: full-bleed support with a controlled overlay when the post needs atmosphere
- Anti-pattern: sterile white backgrounds with no emotional context

## Background Treatment

- Prefer suggestive, image-led compositions
- Use a dark overlay when the image needs legibility
- Preserve texture and warmth
- Avoid flat panels unless the post is explicitly instructional

## Typography Direction

- Headline-first hierarchy
- Clear sans pairings
- Strong legibility on mobile
- Short titles with high contrast
- Avoid tiny type and dense explanatory blocks

## Composition Family

- Editorial
- Proof-led
- Backstage
- Educational
- Organic image-led

## CTA Style

- Prefer save, share, reply or learn-more CTAs
- Avoid hard selling on every piece
- Keep the CTA lightweight when the image already carries the atmosphere

## Do

- Start from the niche and the image story
- Use vibrant but controlled accents
- Let support imagery carry the mood
- Alternate between proof, teaching and backstage logic
- Keep the piece friendly, useful and direct

## Don't

- Do not make the brand feel cold or corporate
- Do not overpack the frame with info blocks
- Do not flatten the art into a generic card
- Do not repeat the same centered composition every time
- Do not rely on text alone

## Batch Guardrails

- Batch should alternate between denser and lighter pieces
- One image-led editorial opening is preferred when the theme allows it
- Proof and support pieces should break repetition after a strong opener
- Keep one accent color dominant per batch so the set feels intentional

## Source Signals

- Official site: https://amiclube.com.br
- Public social presence: Instagram, Facebook, Pinterest, YouTube
- Product and education focus: amigurumi and artisan support

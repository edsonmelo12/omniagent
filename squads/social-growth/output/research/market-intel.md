# Market Intel

Amiclube market synthesis for the current organic social cycle.

## Executive Summary

- Amiclube sits at the intersection of amigurumi, artisan commerce and practical education for small handmade businesses.
- The audience needs help with pricing, selling, organizing offers and posting consistently across channels.
- The strongest angle is practical business guidance for artisans, not generic inspiration.
- Instagram and Facebook are the main trust and conversion channels, while TikTok and Pinterest support discovery.
- The content strategy should connect products, education and proof into a repeatable editorial system.

## Audience

- Primary segment: artisans and women entrepreneurs who already make amigurumi or other handmade products.
- Secondary segment: beginners who want to understand how to sell, price and present their work online.
- Main pains: low visibility, inconsistent sales, uncertainty about pricing, content fatigue and lack of structure.
- Content habits: short practical tips, visual proof, step-by-step guidance, product showcases and educational posts.

## Competitive Landscape

- Most comparable accounts in the market fall into three buckets:
  - craft-only profiles focused on products and aesthetics;
  - generic marketing profiles that do not understand the artisan context;
  - isolated educational pages that explain one topic but do not connect it to an offer.
- Amiclube stands out by combining catalog, education and practical commercial guidance for artisans.

## Opportunities

1. Build recurring educational series around pricing, selling, organization and content for artisans.
2. Translate catalog items into content pillars, seasonal campaigns and product-based storytelling.
3. Use social proof, process content and visual demonstrations to reduce buyer uncertainty.
4. Create content that helps artisans move from hobby behavior to a more structured business mindset.
5. Prioritize formats that show utility fast, especially carousels, reels and short posts.

## Risks and Assumptions

- Assumption: the strongest resonance will come from practical advice, not generic inspiration.
- Risk: if the catalog is not clearly organized, content may become too broad and lose focus.
- Risk: TikTok should stay secondary until the redirect and channel reliability are validated.
- Risk: the team may need to separate education content from direct product promotion to avoid mixed messages.

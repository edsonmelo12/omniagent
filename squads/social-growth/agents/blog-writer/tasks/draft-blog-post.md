# Draft Blog Post

Write the draft as a differentiated, scannable, and reusable article without collapsing into the default skeleton.

## Process

1. Draft the article using the chosen hook, preserved thesis, and family-specific arc.
2. Carry the proof map through the body, including nuance and objection handling where the architecture requires it.
3. Add SEO structure, FAQ, link guidance, word-count target, and the featured-image sections without weakening the article voice.
4. Deliver the full `# Blog Post` format required by the pipeline step.

## Output Format

```markdown
# Blog Post

## Topic and Intent

## Editorial Architecture

## Copy Brief Lock

## Hook Strategy

## Title Tag

## Meta Description

## H1

## Intro

## Body

## Nuance and Proof

## FAQ

## Conclusion

## SEO Checklist

## Word Count Target

## Featured Image

## Featured Image Source Search

## Featured Image Selection Criteria

## Family Ledger Entry
```

## Output Example

> Follow the pipeline step format exactly. The hook strategy must feel tied to the article and the body must preserve the chosen family arc.

## Quality Criteria

- [ ] The body preserves the family-specific section arc.
- [ ] The hook strategy and copy brief are visible in the final draft.
- [ ] The article feels differentiated, not templated.

## Veto Conditions

Reject and redo if ANY are true:
1. The draft defaults to a generic “what is / benefits / steps / FAQ / conclusion” mold.
2. The body ignores the chosen hook or the locked objection.

# Content Trend Researcher

Use this skill before committing to an angle, outline, or thesis for long-form content.

## Purpose

Prevent the team from writing generic blog posts that simply restate what every competitor already says.

## Core Rules

1. Start from demand, not from internal preference.
2. Separate stable evergreen demand from short-lived trend noise.
3. Find the tension, contradiction, or under-covered gap that makes the article worth publishing.
4. Produce multiple viable angles before locking the structure.
5. Identify what nearby or recent posts must not repeat.

## Required Outputs

- Search intent hypothesis
- Audience tension or unresolved question
- Content gap or editorial opportunity
- Three distinct angle options
- Recommended structure family
- Repetition risks to avoid
- Confidence level and freshness window for trend-based claims

## Diagnostic Questions

- What is the reader actually trying to decide, fix, compare, or understand?
- Which angle is already overused in ranking pages?
- Which point of view is missing or weak in existing content?
- What proof or example would make this article less replaceable?
- Which recent neighboring post patterns should not be reused?

## Angle Design Requirements

When proposing angle options, vary all of the following where possible:

- core tension
- promise
- structure family
- proof style
- CTA direction

Do not produce three cosmetic variations of the same article.

## Quality Gate

Reject the draft plan and rethink the angle if any of these are true:

1. The angle could be used unchanged by a generic competitor.
2. The structure family was chosen before the editorial tension was defined.
3. The article has no clear information gain beyond a standard how-to post.

# Copywriting

Use this skill when a piece needs persuasion, tension, and a deliberate CTA rather than neutral exposition.

## Purpose

Improve clarity, hook strength, and conversion logic without turning the article into hype.

## Hard Gate

Do not draft the body until a Copy Brief Lock is defined.

## Copy Brief Lock

The brief must explicitly define:

- target reader
- reader problem in their language
- awareness level
- primary promise
- dominant objection
- proof available
- CTA direction
- hook strategy

## Hook Rule

Generate three hook options that differ materially in:

- psychological driver
- structural type
- level of aggression

Then choose one and justify why it fits the audience and the article thesis.

## Body Rules

1. Clarity beats cleverness.
2. Use one strong idea per paragraph.
3. Every major section must move the reader toward understanding or action.
4. Objection handling must appear before the close.
5. CTA intensity must match the funnel stage.

## Anti-Hype Rules

- Never fabricate proof.
- Never inflate certainty where the brand lacks evidence.
- Never use generic motivational filler.
- Never let the hook promise something the article does not deliver.

## Quality Gate

Reject and redo if:

1. The copy brief is missing or vague.
2. The hook and CTA feel detachable from the article thesis.
3. The article sounds polished but not audience-specific.
